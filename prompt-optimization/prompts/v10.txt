Identify every piece of exercise equipment visible in these gym photos.

## Step 1: Scan by zone

Work through the image systematically:
1. Cardio zone — treadmills, ellipticals, bikes, rowing machines, stair climbers. Count each machine by its individual frame and console.
2. Strength machines — functional trainers (dual cable stacks), single cable machines, smith machines, plate-loaded machines.
3. Free weights — dumbbell racks, barbell stations, kettlebells.
4. Benches — check adjustment mechanisms before classifying.
5. Floor/corners — stability balls, medicine balls, foam rollers, yoga mats.
6. Walls/ceiling — pull-up bars, TRX systems, resistance bands.

## Step 2: Classify each item

Use only these types: dumbbell, barbell, cable_machine, smith_machine, bench_flat, bench_incline, bench_adjustable, treadmill, elliptical, rowing_machine, pull_up_bar, resistance_bands, kettlebell, leg_press, lat_pulldown, pec_deck, leg_curl, leg_extension, shoulder_press_machine, chest_press_machine, seated_row, hack_squat, preacher_curl_bench, ab_bench, hyperextension_bench, battle_ropes, trx_suspension, medicine_ball, stability_ball, foam_roller, yoga_mat, stair_climber, stationary_bike, spin_bike, functional_trainer

### Critical distinctions (common error sources)

Functional Trainer vs Cable Machine:
- functional_trainer: TWO separate weight stacks with independent adjustable cable arms (one stack per side)
- cable_machine: ONE central weight stack with pulleys/attachments
- Look specifically for the dual-stack setup — this is commonly missed

Bench Classification (major false positive area):
- bench_flat: ONLY if it's clearly a flat, non-adjustable surface
- bench_adjustable: ONLY if you can see adjustment pins, levers, or multiple angle positions
- bench_incline: ONLY for fixed incline benches
- DO NOT assume adjustability — many flat benches are mistakenly called adjustable

### False positive prevention

Only report equipment you can directly point to in the image:
- pull_up_bar: actual horizontal bar mounted for pull-ups. Structural beams, door frames, and cable crossover top bars do NOT count.
- smith_machine: vertical guided barbell on rails with safety catches. A barbell on a rack is not a smith machine.
- seated_row: dedicated machine with chest pad and horizontal pulling handle. A cable attachment used for rows is NOT a seated_row.
- dumbbell: actual dumbbells or dumbbell racks must be visible. Other weights do not count.
- Do not report equipment that is merely plausible based on gym type. Only report what is visible.

### False negative prevention

These items are commonly missed:
- Functional trainers in corners or against walls — scan for dual cable setups
- Medicine balls in racks, on floors, or in storage areas
- Small equipment (stability balls, foam rollers) under benches or in corners
- Stationary bikes and ellipticals partially behind other equipment

### Counting rules

- Dumbbell rack = count: 1, describe weight range in details
- Kettlebell set = count: 1, describe collection in details
- Medicine ball rack = count: 1, describe quantity in details
- Individual machines: count each unit by distinct frames. Do not estimate.

### Confidence

- high: fully visible and clearly identifiable
- medium: partially obscured but identifiable type
- low: type uncertain or heavily obscured

## Step 3: Verify before output

For each item:
1. Can you point to where it is in the image? If not, remove it.
2. Does it match the classification rules above? If bench, did you verify adjustment hardware?
3. Is the count based on individually distinguishable units, not estimates?
